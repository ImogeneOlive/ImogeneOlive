I often paint birds as a subject, I can't help but enjoy painting wings and feathers! For this piece I really wanted a dynamic, overlapping, enveloping composition, with dark and moody colours. I just felt I wanted to paint something almost gothic and complex. I had a lot of fun with the colour palette, sneaking in shades of rosy reds to contrast the subtly green background. I was drawn to vultures as a subject for their impressive wingspan and complicated layered feathers.
This painting is a fairly big size and I think it would look great as the main focal point of a room to tie together a space of similar colours or theme. I like it so much I want to keep it for myself!

The board this is painted on is strong and thick with a texture that mimics canvas, and allows colours in the underlayer to peek through, creating more depth.
