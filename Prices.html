Tips for providing images

To ensure I can create the best pet portrait for you a good reference photograph is important. To help you choose the best image or take a new one, here are some tips and examples.
- High quality. Please provide the highest quality images you can. This means that a lot of detail is visible and the size of the image is large.
- Good lighting. Try to find or take an image of your pet that has good lighting. This means there are no heavy shadows hiding their features, and that flash was not used on the camera.
- Good pose. Often successful pet photos are taken at eye-level with your animal. If the photograph is taken from above them and looking down it can warp their body shape and make the painting look unnatural.

Do not worry if you are uncertain or have limited photographs available! Please feel free to send as many photographs as you like and we can pick the best one to use together.
